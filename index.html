Effect of Potassium Nitrate on Growth of Rapid Cycling Brassica rapa (Wisconsin Fast Plants) in the Presence of Glyphosate

Abstract

  • Glyphosate is one of the most popular herbicides used worldwide and works by interrupting the shikimate pathway. Potassium nitrate is an effective fertilizer and contains important plant nutrients, such as potassium and nitrogen. This study aimed to see if potassium nitrate could reverse the effects of glyphosate. It was hypothesized that the nitrate content in a moderate amount of potassium nitrate would overcome the effects of glyphosate by allowing the shikimate pathway to function properly again and produce essential amino acids in Rapid-cycling Brassica rapa. The study was conducted by growing 5 groups of 10 Rapid-cycling Brassica rapa and splitting them up. One group was set as a positive control, only being treated with distilled water, and another group was set as a negative control, being treated with both distilled water and glyphosate. The other three groups had glyphosate applied to them as well as varying concentrations of potassium nitrate solutions, with a low concentration (2 g/L), medium concentration (5 g/L), and a high concentration (20 g/L). The length of the plants before and after one week of experimentation is recorded, along with the dry biomass. An ANOVA Statistical Test was run on the change in lengths, and T-tests were run on the data after the ANOVA came back statistically significant. The data supported the hypothesis, as the length of plants in medium and high concentrations of potassium nitrate was statistically significantly longer compared to the negative control. Further, the length of the plants in the high concentration had no statistically significant growth as compared to the positive control, showing that they were similar values. It was found that a moderate or high concentration of potassium nitrate may be able to reverse the effects of glyphosate.

Background

Glyphosate is one of the most popular herbicides used worldwide and works by interrupting the shikimate pathway. Potassium nitrate is an effective fertilizer and contains important plant nutrients, such as potassium and nitrogen. This study aimed to see if potassium nitrate could reverse the effects of glyphosate.

Rationale

While being used, glyphosate can accidentally be introduced to crops. Some crops are engineered to be resistant to glyphosate, allowing farmers to spray their entire field without worrying about it affecting their crops. However, not all crops are genetically engineered. It is known that potassium nitrate has a beneficial effect on plants when used in moderate amounts. If a plant is accidentally introduced to glyphosate, it could die. If it was known that something, in this case, potassium nitrate, could overcome glyphotate, plants could be saved from dying.
